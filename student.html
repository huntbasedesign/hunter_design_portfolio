Student Worker Hub

Est. 2025

Davis / Atwater, CA

November, 11th 2025

Design Brief

Problem Statement

Hunter, a busy college student, needs a way to log their work hour through an app to save time and easily access their timecard, schedule upcoming shifts, and different work resources for contacting managers, employees, and Human Resources.

Target Audience and Personas

Target Audience: Student Employees and University Managers/Bosses 18-24 years old.

Needs: As student workers, we need a space and an app to combine many different aspects of life. For example, they should have a place where students can manage their class/work schedules. The app should also have messaging features to contact other students with questions or trade or drop shifts. There should also be a way to reach out to upper management and human resources regarding training and certifications or just general.

Goal: The app aims to create a seamless process for managing everything in a student's work/school life. So that they can save valuable time, rather than the current alternative of having to go to 4 or 5 different websites to do these same tasks.

Pain Points: The main pain point is that these features are only offered through browser searches, and currently, no version condenses them into a single app.

Research Summary

Competitive Market

Many different types of apps tackle these specific problems for companies. Still, these current app companies would translate poorly to the scale at which this project would need to be done to provide service for an entire university workforce like UC Davis. Instead, taking inspiration from some of the apps' good features could help recreate the app for a more extensive and diverse employee base.

Some opportunities include integrating previously created features for students all in one place. For example, incorporating the payroll, scheduling, messaging, and outreach systems, currently on different platforms, would streamline and simplify the students' experience.

Some pain points for the projects will be ensuring that the user understands all the different options and features of the app. So, it will be essential to categorize everything and ensure the navigation is clear for students to understand.

Equity Consideration

The main equity concern this would cover is financial. This app will help with time management and provide resources for work or payment problems. It will also make it easier and more transparent for students to get paid and have the network to sign up for shifts or contact other employees to trade and find more work.

Features

  • Timecrading Calendar System
  • Shift Sign Up / Drop or Trade Calendar: time management by displaying their class schedule with their work schedule.
  • Messaging System to contact Managers and other employees
  • Human Resources for students budgeting/talking to a professional about workplace problems.time management by having their class schedule display with their work schedule.
