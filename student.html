Design Brief

Problem Statement

Hunter, a busy college student, needs a way to log their work hours through an app to save time and easily access their timecard, schedule upcoming shifts, and use different work resources for contacting managers, employees, and Human Resources.

Target Audience & Personas

Student employees and university managers aged 18–24 who need a single space to manage the overlapping demands of work and school.

Goal

Create a seamless process for managing everything in a student's work and school life — saving valuable time instead of navigating four or five separate websites to accomplish the same tasks.

The main pain point is that these features are currently only available through scattered browser searches, with no single app condensing them for student workers.

Features

Four core systems work together to cover every aspect of a student worker's day.

01

Timecarding System

Log work hours directly in the app with a calendar-based interface — no more browser logins or paper timesheets.

02

Shift Scheduling

Sign up, drop, or trade shifts with a unified calendar that displays class and work schedules side by side.

03

Messaging System

Contact managers and fellow employees directly — coordinate shift trades, ask questions, and stay connected without leaving the app.

04

Human Resources

Access budgeting tools and connect with HR professionals for workplace concerns, training, certifications, and general support.

Research Summary

Competitive Market

Many apps tackle pieces of this problem for private companies, but none scale to the size and diversity of a university workforce like UC Davis. Rather than copying existing solutions, this project draws inspiration from their strongest features and rebuilds them for a broader, more varied employee base.

Key opportunities include integrating payroll, scheduling, messaging, and HR outreach — currently scattered across different platforms — into a single streamlined experience. The primary design challenge is ensuring students can immediately understand all available options, making clear categorization and intuitive navigation essential.

Equity Consideration

Financial Accessibility

The app's core equity focus is financial. By making pay transparency, shift access, and HR resources easier to reach, it lowers barriers for students who depend on their income — ensuring they can get paid accurately, find more work, and resolve workplace issues without friction.
